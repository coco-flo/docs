## The core value proposition

Most service business owners are stuck between two bad options.

Simple mobile apps (Invoice Simple, Wave, Invoice Fly) — cheap and fast, but stop at the invoice. No job scheduling, no team, no follow-up, no signed agreement.

Full field service platforms (Jobber $19–$529/mo, HouseCall Pro $79–$329/mo) — built for dispatchers, GPS routing, crew management. Wrong product for a solo or small team. You pay for complexity you'll never use.

Clarely fills the gap: everything the simple apps can't do, without the overhead of platforms built for businesses ten times your size.

**The pitch order:**

1. Built for your exact stage — not too simple, not too big
2. One workflow, quote to paid, on your phone, priced for a one-person operation
3. Quotes include a legally binding signature that freezes scope — no competitor offers this at any price

Scope protection answers "how is your quote different from Invoice Simple?" — it is not the opening argument.

## The pitch in one sentence

There's nothing between the simple invoice apps and Jobber — Clarely fills that gap, and every quote includes a client signature that permanently freezes the scope.

## Who you're talking to

A solo or small service business owner who has outgrown texting quotes but can't justify $99–$329/month. Has clients who sometimes dispute what was agreed. Has team members to coordinate without a full dispatch system. Runs everything from their phone.

**Cleaners:** Too big for Invoice Simple but Jobber is \$99\+/month for features a solo cleaner doesn't need. Clarely gives you signed quotes, job scheduling, team access, and invoice follow-up at a price built for a one-person operation.

## Handling objections

**"I already use Jobber / HouseCall Pro."**

> "Those are built for larger operations. If you're a small team not using dispatch and GPS, you're paying for a lot you don't need. Clarely does what a service business owner actually uses day to day, at a fraction of the cost."

**"I already use Invoice Simple / Wave."**

> "Those are fine up to a point. Once you have regular clients, a team member, or jobs to schedule — they fall short. Clarely picks up exactly where those apps stop."

**"I just do it over text."**

> "That works until a client disputes what was agreed. The first time it happens without written proof, it costs you time or money."

**"What about Jobber's \$19/month plan?"**

> "Jobber Core at $19/month has no team management, limited scheduling, and no signed scope protection. Features a growing operation needs start at $99/month. Clarely gives you more than Jobber Core for \$20/month."

**"\$20/month seems like a lot."**

> "$20/month is less than one hour of your labor. Standard pricing is $79/month when we open up."

**"What if Clarely shuts down?"**

> "'For as long as Clarely operates this plan' is honest, not a dodge. This is a sustainable business at \$20/month — not a VC-funded product burning cash to grow."

**"I'll wait until it's more established."**

> "Standard pricing is \$79/month when we open up. The founder program is for people who want to shape it early."

## Group channel tone

Direct and informal. Write the way you talk.

- Share what's shipping before it ships: "Pushed a fix for the job completion notification bug. Let me know if you see it again."
- Acknowledge bugs immediately: "Saw the report. Looking at it now. Update in a couple hours."
- Ask direct questions: "Anyone tried recurring jobs yet? Curious if it works the way you'd expect."
- Don't over-communicate. One message that matters beats three that don't.
