Recurring jobs let you set up a repeating schedule for clients who need regular service — weekly cleaning, monthly maintenance, quarterly inspections, and so on.

## Setting up a recurring job

Start with a regular job that's already in your system. You can make any existing job recurring from the job detail page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the job">
    Go to **Jobs** and open the job you want to make recurring.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make it recurring">
    Tap **Make Recurring** on the job detail page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the schedule">
    Choose the frequency: weekly, biweekly, every 3 weeks, every 4 weeks, monthly, or custom interval. Set how many future occurrences Clarely should generate ahead.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set an end condition (optional)">
    Choose when the series ends: no end date, after a set number of occurrences, or on a specific date. Leave it open-ended for ongoing service.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Tap **Save**. Clarely creates the upcoming occurrences automatically and keeps generating new ones on a rolling basis.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How recurring jobs work

Each occurrence is a separate job in your schedule. Clarely generates them on a rolling window — as you complete jobs, new ones are added to keep your calendar populated ahead of schedule.

If auto-invoice is enabled and the job is linked to an accepted quote, completing each occurrence can automatically create a draft invoice for that service period.

## Editing a recurring series

Open any job in the series and tap **Edit Series** to make changes to the schedule — frequency, time, or day of week. Changes apply to the series going forward from the point you edit.

To change the scope or pricing of a recurring engagement, cancel the series and create a new quote with the updated terms. See [Scope protection](/quotes/scope-protection).

## Cancelling a recurring series

Open any job in the series and tap **Manage Recurrence**, then choose **Cancel Series**. This cancels all remaining scheduled jobs in the series. The series and its completed jobs remain in your records.

To cancel a single occurrence without affecting the rest of the series, open that individual job and cancel it directly from the job detail page.
