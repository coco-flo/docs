## Why this matters

At 25 founders, every person matters. Losing one is a 4% churn rate. Losing three is a product signal. The goal is to catch fixable problems before they become cancellations, and to learn from the ones that aren't fixable.

## Early warning signals

**Usage signals:**

- Hasn't sent a quote in 4\+ weeks after being previously active
- App login frequency dropping significantly
- Not completing jobs or invoicing after creating quotes
- Not using team members despite being set up

**Engagement signals:**

- Missing 2\+ consecutive monthly surveys without reaching out
- Going quiet in the group channel after previously being active
- Short or disengaged survey responses ("fine" / "no feedback")
- Not responding to follow-up messages within 48 hours

**Explicit signals:**

- Mentions switching to another tool
- Complains about a specific missing feature multiple times
- Asks about pricing more than once
- Says they're "too busy" to continue

## How to intervene early

**For usage drops:**

> "\[Name\], I noticed you haven't sent a quote recently. Everything okay? Is there something that got in the way of using Clarely, or is it just a slow period for business?"

Listen first. If it's a product problem, fix it or explain the roadmap. If it's a slow period, acknowledge it and stay in touch.

**For survey non-response:**

> "\[Name\], I saw you haven't had a chance to fill out the \[Month X\] survey. No pressure — just want to make sure you're getting value from Clarely and nothing is broken. How's it going?"

**For repeated feature requests:**

> "You've mentioned \[feature\] a few times. I want to be direct: it's on the roadmap but not in the immediate next release. If that feature is important enough to affect whether you keep using Clarely, I want to know that now rather than at month 6."

## The retention conversation

When a founder signals they're considering leaving:

1. **Ask before pitching.** "What's driving that?" Find the real reason before responding.
2. **Don't make promises you can't keep.** If a feature isn't coming soon, say so. Honesty beats over-promising.
3. **Separate product problems from fit problems.** Product problems might be fixable. Fit problems probably aren't.
4. **If it's a bug or UX issue:** Fix it and tell them. "That was a real problem and here's what I did to fix it" is a strong retention message.
5. **If they're leaving anyway:** Ask for a brief conversation about why. "I'd just like to understand what Clarely was missing for you. It'll help me build it better."

## When a founder churns

1. Update cohort tracker: Status = Cancelled, date, reason
2. Founder Rate: cancel the Stripe subscription at the appropriate time
3. Free Forever: no billing action
4. Create a CLA- or OPS- issue with Founder Feedback label documenting the reason
5. Data is retained unless they request deletion
6. Send a brief exit message:
   > "\[Name\], I've updated your account. Your data is still there if you ever want to come back. Thanks for the time you gave to the program — the feedback mattered."

## Learning from churn

After every churn, answer and add to the cohort tracker notes:

1. What was the stated reason?
2. What was the real reason (based on survey data, usage patterns, conversations)?
3. Was this preventable? What would have prevented it?
4. Is this a product gap, a communication failure, or a fit problem?
5. Does this match a pattern from previous churn?

If the same reason appears 3\+ times, it's a product or positioning issue. Route to Prod.
