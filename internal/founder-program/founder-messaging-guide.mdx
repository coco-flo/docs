## The core value proposition

Most service business owners are stuck between two bad options.

On one side: simple mobile apps — Invoice Simple, Wave, Invoice Fly. Cheap and fast, but they stop at the invoice. No job scheduling, no team, no follow-up, no signed agreement. Fine when you're starting out. Not enough once you have real clients and a real workflow.

On the other side: full field service platforms — Jobber ($19–$529/month), HouseCall Pro ($79–$329/month). Built for companies with dispatchers, GPS routing, crew management, and job costing. Wrong product for a solo operator or small team. You end up paying for complexity you'll never use.

Clarely fills the gap. Everything you can't do in a simple invoicing app — signed quotes, job scheduling, team access, automated follow-up — without the overhead of platforms built for businesses ten times your size. Built for one person running a real service business from their phone.

**The pitch order:**

1. This was built for your exact stage — not too simple, not too big
2. One workflow, quote to paid, on your phone, priced for a one-person operation
3. The quotes include a legally binding signature that freezes scope — something no competitor offers at any price

Scope protection is what makes Clarely's quote better than any other tool's quote. It is not the opening argument. It answers "how is your quote different from Invoice Simple?" — not "why should I use this?"

## The pitch in one sentence

There's nothing between the simple invoice apps and Jobber — Clarely fills that gap, and every quote includes a client signature that permanently freezes the scope.

## Who you're talking to

A solo or small service business owner who:

- Has outgrown texting quotes but isn't ready for or can't justify a $99–$329/month platform
- Has clients who sometimes dispute what was agreed, or knows it's only a matter of time
- Has team members they're trying to coordinate without a full dispatch system
- Runs everything from their phone

They don't want another enterprise tool. They want something that does what they actually need, set up in under ten minutes.

## Handling objections

**"I already use Jobber / HouseCall Pro."**

> "Those are built for larger operations — dispatching, GPS, crew management, job costing. If you're a small team and not using that stack, you're paying for a lot you don't need. Clarely does what a service business owner actually uses day to day, at a price that makes sense for a one-person operation."

**"I already use Invoice Simple / Wave."**

> "Those are fine up to a point. Once you have regular clients, a team member, or jobs you need to schedule — they fall short. Clarely picks up exactly where those apps stop."

**"I just do it over text."**

> "That works until a client disputes what was agreed. The first time it happens without written proof, it costs you time or money. Clarely takes about the same effort as sending a detailed text, and you end up with a signed record."

**"What about Jobber's \$19/month plan?"**

> "Jobber Core at $19/month gets you basic quoting and invoicing but no team management, limited scheduling, and no signed scope protection. The features a growing operation actually needs start at $99/month. Clarely gives you more than Jobber Core for \$20/month, without locking you into a platform built for a much bigger business."

**"\$20/month seems like a lot."**

> "$20/month is less than one hour of your labor. Standard pricing when we open up is $79/month. The \$20 is permanent for the 25 founders."

**"What if Clarely shuts down?"**

> "'For as long as Clarely operates this plan' is honest, not a dodge. This is a sustainable business at \$20/month — not a VC-funded product burning cash to grow."

**"I'll wait until it's more established."**

> "That's fair. Standard pricing is \$79/month when we open up. The founder program is for people who want to shape it early. If you'd rather wait and pay standard pricing, that's a perfectly reasonable call."

## Group channel communication tone

Direct and informal. Write the way you talk, not the way a company newsletter reads.

- Share what's shipping before it ships: "Pushed a fix for the job completion notification bug. Let me know if you see it again."
- Acknowledge bugs immediately: "Saw the report. Looking at it now. Update in a couple hours."
- Ask direct questions: "Anyone tried recurring jobs yet? Curious if it works the way you'd expect."
- Don't over-communicate. One message that matters beats three that don't.
