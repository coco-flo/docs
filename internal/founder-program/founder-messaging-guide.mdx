## The core value proposition

Most service business owners are stuck between two bad options.

On one side: simple mobile apps — Invoice Simple, Wave, Invoice Fly. Cheap and fast, but they stop at the invoice. No job scheduling, no team, no follow-up, no signed agreement.

On the other side: full field service platforms — Jobber ($19–$529/month), HouseCall Pro ($79–$329/month). Built for companies with dispatchers, GPS routing, crew management, and job costing. Wrong product for a solo operator or small team.

Clarely fills the gap. Everything you can't do in a simple invoicing app, without the overhead of platforms built for businesses ten times your size.

**The pitch order:**

1. Built for your exact stage — not too simple, not too big
2. One workflow, quote to paid, on your phone, priced for a one-person operation
3. Quotes include a legally binding signature that freezes scope — no competitor offers this at any price

Scope protection answers "how is your quote different from Invoice Simple?" — it is not the opening argument.

## The pitch in one sentence

There's nothing between the simple invoice apps and Jobber — Clarely fills that gap, and every quote includes a client signature that permanently freezes the scope.

## Who you're talking to

A solo or small service business owner who:

- Has outgrown texting quotes but can't justify $99–$329/month for features they won't use
- Has clients who sometimes dispute what was agreed, or knows it's only a matter of time
- Has team members to coordinate without a full dispatch system
- Runs everything from their phone

## Handling objections

**"I already use Jobber / HouseCall Pro."**

> "Those are built for larger operations. If you're a small team not using dispatching or GPS, you're paying for a lot you don't need. Clarely does what you actually use day to day at a price that fits."

**"I already use Invoice Simple / Wave."**

> "Those work up to a point. Once you have regular clients, a team member, or jobs to schedule — they fall short. Clarely picks up where those apps stop."

**"I just do it over text."**

> "That works until a client disputes what was agreed. Clarely takes about the same effort as a detailed text, and you end up with a signed record."

**"What about Jobber's \$19/month plan?"**

> "Jobber Core gets you basic quoting and invoicing but no team management, limited scheduling, and no signed scope protection. Real features start at $99/month. Clarely gives you more than Jobber Core for $20/month."

**"\$20/month seems like a lot."**

> "$20/month is less than one hour of your labor. Standard pricing when we open up is $79/month. The \$20 is permanent for the 25 founders."

**"What if Clarely shuts down?"**

> "'For as long as Clarely operates this plan' is honest, not a dodge. This is a sustainable business at \$20/month — not a VC-funded product burning cash to grow."

**"I'll wait until it's more established."**

> "That's fair. Standard pricing is \$79/month when we open up. The founder program is for people who want to shape it early."

## Group channel tone

Direct and informal. Write the way you talk.

- Ship updates before they're announced publicly: "Pushed a fix for the job completion bug. Let me know if you see it again."
- Acknowledge bugs immediately: "On it. Update in a couple hours."
- Ask direct questions: "Anyone tried recurring jobs yet? Curious if it works the way you'd expect."
- Don't over-communicate. One message that matters beats three that don't.
