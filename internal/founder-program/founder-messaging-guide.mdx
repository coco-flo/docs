## The pitch in one sentence

Clarely is the only quoting tool that freezes scope when your client signs, so you can't be pushed to do extra work for free, and we're giving 25 business owners permanent pricing before we open up.

## Who you're talking to

The target is a solo or small service business owner who:

- Has clients who sometimes dispute what was agreed
- Runs their business "over text" (quoting via message, invoicing via Venmo request)
- Has no formal quoting process, or uses something they outgrew (Wave, Jobber free tier)
- Has team members they're trying to coordinate

They are not impressed by "enterprise features" or "best-in-class UX." They care about getting paid, avoiding disputes, and looking professional.

## Cold outreach opening

For Facebook group / Reddit / WhatsApp group cold outreach:

> "Quick question for anyone who runs a \[cleaning / trades / service\] business: how do you handle it when a client says you didn't do something that was on the quote? I built a tool that locks scope the second a client signs, so that argument can't happen. Working with 25 business owners before opening it up. If you send quotes, it might be worth a look."

Or more direct:

> "I'm looking for \[cleaning / landscaping / trades\] business owners to try Clarely before launch. You get full access free for 6 months, then \$20/month permanently. Only asking for monthly check-ins in return. DM me if you're interested."

## The value proposition by audience

**Cleaners:** You send a quote that says 3-bedroom deep clean. Client says you didn't clean the oven. You don't have it in writing. Clarely freezes exactly what you quoted the second they sign. That conversation stops before it starts.

**Tradespeople:** Scope creep costs you money on every job. A client who agreed to a quoted scope can't add to it after the fact without a new quote. Clarely makes that a documented fact, not a disagreement.

**Professional services:** Your client signed a statement of work but now wants revisions outside the original scope. The signed Clarely quote is your record of what was agreed.

## Handling objections

**"I already use Jobber / HouseCall Pro / QuickBooks."**

> "None of those platforms lock scope when a client signs. You can still be asked to do more work for the quoted price. Clarely is the only tool that freezes it. If you've never had that problem, you might not need it. If you have, it's worth 10 minutes."

**"I just do it over text / verbally."**

> "That works until it doesn't. The first time a client disputes what was agreed and you don't have a signed document, that conversation is going to cost you time or money. Clarely makes it take less time than what you're doing now."

**"I'm not a tech person."**

> "It's designed for people who run their business from their phone. You build a quote in a few taps, send a link, they sign with their name. It's less complicated than the app you use to pay your own invoices."

**"\$20/month seems like a lot."**

> "$20/month is less than one hour of your labor. If a signed quote prevents one scope dispute a year, it paid for itself. Standard pricing when we open up is $79/month. The \$20 is permanent for founders."

**"What if Clarely shuts down?"**

> "'For as long as Clarely operates this plan' is an honest statement, not a dodge. I'm not going to make a commitment I can't keep. What I can tell you is that this is a sustainable business at \$20/month, not a VC-funded growth-at-all-costs product."

**"I'll wait until it's more established."**

> "That's fair. Standard pricing is \$79/month when we open up. The founder program is specifically for people who want to be part of shaping the product while it's early. If you'd rather wait and pay standard pricing, that's totally reasonable."

## Group channel communication tone

The founder group channel is direct and informal. Write the way you talk, not the way a company newsletter is written.

- Share what's shipping before it ships: "Pushed a fix for the job completion notification bug this morning. Let me know if you see it again."
- Acknowledge bugs immediately: "Saw the report. Looking at it now. I'll update in a couple hours."
- Ask direct questions: "Anyone tried the recurring jobs feature yet? Curious if it works the way you'd expect."
- Don't over-communicate: Quality over frequency. One message that matters is better than three that don't.

## Survey follow-up language

When sending a monthly survey:

> "\[Month X\] survey is live. 5 questions, less than 5 minutes. Your feedback directly shapes what gets built next. \[Link\]"

When a founder gives particularly useful feedback:

> "This is exactly the kind of thing I need to hear. Adding it to the roadmap conversation."

When a founder reports a bug:

> "On it. I'll follow up here once it's resolved."
