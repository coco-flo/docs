Scope protection is the defining feature of Clarely. It's what separates Clarely from every other quoting tool on the market.

## The problem it solves

Without a signed, frozen quote, disputes happen constantly. A client says they asked for more than you delivered. You say you delivered exactly what was discussed. Neither of you has a clear record of what was agreed. You end up doing extra work for free or having a difficult conversation with a client.

Clarely eliminates that problem.

## How scope protection works

When a client signs a quote in Clarely, the system freezes an exact snapshot of every line item — the description, quantity, price, and total — at that moment. This snapshot is stored permanently and is tamper-proof.

If a client later claims they expected something different, you open the signed quote and show them exactly what they agreed to, with their signature and the timestamp.

## What it protects you from

- Clients asking for additional work at no charge after signing
- Disputes about what services were included in the price
- "That's not what I thought I was getting" conversations
- Verbal agreements that are hard to prove

## What it doesn't cover

Scope protection applies to what was in the quote. If you verbally agree to add something after the quote is signed, that addition isn't protected. For any scope change after signing, create a new quote for the additional work.

## Competitors don't offer this

Jobber, HouseCall Pro, and every other platform at any price point let clients dispute scope after the fact. Clarely is the only platform where the scope is legally frozen at signature. This is a hard differentiator — not a feature that can be matched by a software update.
