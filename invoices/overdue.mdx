An invoice becomes overdue when it passes its due date without being paid. Clarely flags overdue invoices so nothing slips through.

## How overdue is determined

Clarely marks an invoice overdue the day after its due date passes. The default due date is 30 days from the invoice send date. You can set a custom due date on any invoice before sending.

## Finding overdue invoices

Overdue invoices are flagged in your invoice list with an **Overdue** badge. You can filter your invoice list by status to see all overdue invoices at once.

## Following up

To follow up on an overdue invoice, open it and tap **Resend**. Clarely resends the invoice email with the payment link. The email subject line indicates the invoice is overdue.

## Marking overdue invoices paid

If a client pays outside of Clarely (cash, check, bank transfer), mark the invoice paid manually. Open the invoice and tap **Mark as Paid**. Enter the payment method and date received.

## Writing off an invoice

If an invoice won't be collected, you can mark it as **Written Off** to remove it from your active overdue list. This is for record-keeping only — it doesn't affect any payments or your Stripe account.

<Tip>
  Staying on top of overdue invoices is easier when you check your invoice list at the end of each week. Set a recurring reminder in your calendar until it becomes habit.
</Tip>
